Library

November 8, 2012

As we turn the page to mid-fall, it is not just about books in the BMS Library. Orientations, online research, digital citizenship, and, of course, many students checking out books have kept us busy these first two months of school. Schedules changed a lot because of testing, and there was more time to read!

Here are the highlights of what’s “new and good” at the BMS Library Media Center.

Did you know that there are alternatives to Google that can help fine-tune your child’s research skills?

Check out our latest online subscription, Grolier Online, which provides an online encyclopedia with primary documents and multimedia references. The website can also be accessed through the BMS Library website under “Research Resources.” A username and password is required. Please e-mail Mrs. Rousseau-Evans for more details.

If you’re looking for a popular news or academic article from your favorite magazine, you have access to approximately 500 popular titles, 35 national and international newspapers, ERIC, NoveList (great for book club books), and a consumer health database through EBSCO. The site can also be accessed by visiting the BMS Library’s website, selecting “Research Resources,” and clicking on EBSCO. Ask your child for the password or e-mail Mrs. Rousseau-Evans.

Also, we have a new way to search for books in the library using BookSystems! One can use this library automation software by accessing the BMS Library webpage and then clicking on “Library Catalog.” While conducting their search for a good read (or a good listen), students can now see what others are reading as the “What’s Hot” covers of books scroll by on the screen. Soon readers will be able to determine which books they have out and write reviews of books in the catalog to share their own good reads (or not so good).

Mark your calendars for November 13-19, 2012!

Our Scholastic Book Fair is coming! The Annual Fall Book Fair family event will be held in conjunction with the Fall Band Concert on the evening of November 15. The fair, a “Reading Road Trip,” will run throughout the week. Remember, you can also shop online.

What BMS Students are checking out from the Library this fall:

  • You Wouldn’t Want to be a Sumerian Slave: a life of hard labor you’d rather avoid by Jacqueline Morley (non-fiction)
  • Smile by Raina Telgemeier (graphic novel)
  • Life as We Knew It by Susan Beth Peiffer (science fiction)

Digital Citizenship

One of the themes of Digital Citizenship taught during extra specials once a week is With Power Comes Responsibility. The power of the internet includes the ability to be anonymous, to create amazing media such as videos, photos, and music, as well as use a new “language” of texting which can be a great way to strengthen relationships but can also lead to excessive use of technology. Click here for the top ten tips presented by Common Sense Media, a web site for parents, educators and teens.

It is a good rule after reading a new book, never to allow yourself another new one till you have read an old one in between.


- C.S. Lewis
