Paws to Read

The Bow School District and Baker Free Library teamed up over the summer for our first, joint summer reading program, Paws to Read. Families read over the summer and kept track of how much they read on their “Paws to Read” logs. A goal of 3,000 hours was set, and several sponsors — Bow Family Dentistry, Howard and Dionne CPAs, Century 21 Circa 72, Granite State Gymnastics, and Northeast Delta Dental — offered to make donations to the Concord-Merrimack County Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals if the goal was reached. Over 4,545 hours were logged, exceeding our goal by almost 1,500 hours! A special presentation was made during the BMS Autumn Fest Assembly, and a check in the amount of $1,208.90 was awarded to the SPCA. Many sponsors were in attendance. Thank you, Bow readers and sponsors, for your participation in this reading initiative.

Mr. Hebert wins!

Imagine Mr. Mike’s surprise when he saw the winning numbers: 1, 17, 42, 46, 55; Megaball: 1. He had matches on 4 of the White Balls AND the Mega Ball!

On Tuesday, October 23, Mike Hebert, our daytime custodian, decided to spend $5 on a Mega Millions® play slip of 5 different plays. He chose his own numbers for 3 of the games and decided to let the lottery terminal randomly select the numbers for the other two (wise decision!). Had the computer selected a 9 instead of a 42, it would have resulted in a $13 million jackpot! No worries. Mr. Mike is still smiling over his $10,000 winnings. Congratulations, Mike!
