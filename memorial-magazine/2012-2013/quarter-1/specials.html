Technology Education

The Bow Memorial Industrial Technology Department hosted another successful summer Engineering Camp during the week of July 30-August 3, 2012. Engineering Camp, a weeklong half-day activity, is a fun introduction to engineering for boys and girls who are interested in finding out more about engineering and related fields. The participants solved a variety of engineering problems by creating solutions as they worked with students from Project Lead the Way, a Bow High School engineering class, as well as some BHS instructors.

This is the third year that Bow Memorial will sponsor two teams in the First Lego League. Both teams are currently hard at work on this year’s themed challenge “Senior Solutions.” There is much work to be done in a short amount of time. This year’s qualifying event will be held at Bishop Brady High School on November 17, 2012.

The BMS Industrial Technology Department continues the process of implementing the latest architectural, Computer Aided Design and Drafting (CADD), and now new this year, Computer Integrated Manufacturing (CIM), software into the curriculum, while still reassuring the need for a “hands on” approach. The department’s goal is to provide an up-to-date facility and curriculum that will enhance critical thinking and challenge the creativity of the students at BMS.

Curtis Edwards, Technology Education

Computer Literacy

What constitutes a strong password? What is phishing? What is pharming? What is vishing? How can digital citizens detect spam/malware and social engineering techniques?

Mr. Roy Bailey, our district’s technology coordinator, was recently a guest speaker in 8th grade. He answered these questions for students in Mrs. Hall’s computer literacy class and Mrs. Rousseau-Evans’s digital citizenship lab. Students were taught the importance of using secure, strong, and multiple passwords. They watched and listened intently as our computer guru showed examples of spam/malware on the district’s email server and described different social engineering techniques (e.g., phishing, pharming, vishing) used to deceive people into performing actions for the purpose of information gathering, fraud, or computer system access.

Students are now better equipped to recognize these fraudulent techniques and understand the importance of cybersecurity. Thank you, Mr. Bailey, for sharing your expertise with us!

Muriel Hall, Computer Literacy
